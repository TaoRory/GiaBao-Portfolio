2022
Ward 12, HCMC · Summer Volunteer Campaign

Hoa Phượng Đỏ — Vice Commander

Vice commander of the ward-level summer volunteer movement. Coordinating student volunteers across neighborhood service, civic education, and outreach — the first time I was responsible for people who weren't from my school, in places that weren't my own.

The summer taught me something the competitions never had. That there is a particular kind of quiet joy in standing behind the smile of a child who has been overlooked, or a grandparent who has run out of strength to work. The work is invisible from there. That's the point.

2024
TH28 · TH29 · TH30 · Annual charity gala

Tuổi Hồng Minh Khai — Head of Artists

One of seven core organizers of an event drawing over three hundred staff — student volunteers from high schools across HCMC. As Head of Artists, I shaped the artistic direction of one of the most well-known student charity galas in the city.

The first time tickets went on sale, we sold four thousand of them in two minutes. The whole arena, gone, before most people had finished refreshing the page.

Every profit from Tuổi Hồng goes back into community work. Across thirty-one years, that has meant hundreds of scholarships for students in difficult circumstances, and dozens of community projects — a playground for children in District 3, milk and warm clothes for ethnic minority children in Gia Lai, and a 90-million-VND "Friendship House" built for a student in Đồng Tháp in October 2025.

The concert is the part that sells out in two minutes. The houses that get built afterward are the part that lasts.

City Level · 946 qualifiers from 2,000+ projects

HCM Science & Technology Competition

This project is a machine that transforms plastic bottles into plastic filaments for 3D printing. The idea stemmed from a simple observation — every week our school's recycling bins overflow with unused plastic bottles, and every week the 3D printers in our school's STEAM labs run out of 3D printing material, costing a lot of money to buy from outside suppliers.

Gold · City 1st Prize
Engineering was never the point. People were. The work just happened to look like building things.
